# H053 — literature review

Pre-registration companion to [design.md](design.md). Written 2026-04-28; freezes the literature consulted at design time. Subsequent additions go to a dated successor file, not back-edits to this one.

Per [~/.claude/CLAUDE.md §Evidence Hierarchy](../../../../.claude/CLAUDE.md), every claim cites a peer-reviewed paper, a working paper with a primary identifier (DOI / arXiv / SSRN / JMLR URL), or an official document. References that I could not anchor to a primary source are listed under §G with `UNVERIFIED:` and are NOT used in the design.md mechanism paragraph.

## Scope

H053 tests whether a snapshot of three chart timeframes (daily ≥3 months, hourly several days 24/7, 5-and-15-min 24-48h) captured at 09:45 ET predicts the cumulative log return of the 09:45–10:30 ET window of the RTH session on ES / NQ / micro futures, with the 09:30–09:45 ET opening 15-min candle treated as a candidate mediator. The user-facing deliverable is a partitioned bias-target-probability table whose rows are pre-registered opening-candle archetypes and whose entries are conditional empirical-frequency estimates with bootstrap CIs.

This review covers seven topics: (A) overnight-to-open and first-hour intraday return mechanisms; (B) multi-timeframe technical features for short-horizon prediction; (C) mediation analysis (formal and applied); (D) chart-pattern archetype classification; (E) probability calibration for the lookup table; (F) LLM-as-feature for intraday prediction; (G) walk-forward / bootstrap / SPA inference. References I could not verify are in §H.

## A. Overnight-to-open and first-hour intraday return mechanisms

- [Andersen, Bollerslev, Diebold & Labys 2003, *Econometrica* 71(2):579–625](https://doi.org/10.1111/1468-0262.00418) — long-memory ARFIMA modelling of realized variance; the U-shaped intraday seasonality is filtered as a nuisance, not introduced here. (Round-1 lit-check correction: original draft attributed the U-shape to ABDL 2003; that documentation is in earlier work — see Wood-McInish-Ord 1985 below and [Andersen & Bollerslev 1997, *Journal of Empirical Finance* 4(2-3):115–158](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0927-5398(97)00004-2) "Intraday periodicity and volatility persistence in financial markets.")
- [Andersen & Bollerslev 1998, *International Economic Review* 39(4):885–905](https://doi.org/10.2307/2527343) — validation of conditional-volatility models using ex-post realized variance built from intraday returns; the conceptual seed of the realized-variance-from-high-frequency-returns literature later formalized in ABDL 2003. The estimator anchor in H053 §3 is ABDL 2003 (and AB 1998 for the seed reference).
- [Wood, McInish & Ord 1985, *Journal of Finance* 40(3):723–739](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1985.tb04996.x) — first-hour and last-hour return seasonality on NYSE; establishes the first-hour as a structurally distinct subwindow.
- [Heston, Korajczyk & Sadka 2010, *Journal of Finance* 65(4):1369–1407](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2010.01573.x) — cross-sectional intraday return periodicity at half-hour intervals; documents a same-time-of-day return continuation that is informative for a fixed-clock-time predictand.
- [Lou, Polk & Skouras 2019, *Journal of Financial Economics* 134(1):192–213](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2019.03.011) — overnight vs intraday return tug-of-war; documents same-period overnight continuation with cross-period reversal, motivating the opening-bar-as-mediator framing. (Round-1 lit-check correction: the original draft cited the wrong DOI 10.1016/j.jfineco.2019.04.001, which resolves to a different JFE 134 paper; corrected to 03.011.)
- [Berkman, Koch, Tuttle & Zhang 2012, *Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis* 47(4):715–741](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0022109012000270) — overnight-to-day return reversal driven by individual-investor attention; ties opening-window microstructure to a measurable mediator channel.

**Synthesis.** Equity-index intraday returns are seasonal at the half-hour grid (HKS 2010); the open is a concentrated information event that transmits overnight information into the intraday session at session-aggregate scale (LPS 2019, BKTZ 2012). Whether the opening-15-min-bar bundles those channels at sub-half-hour resolution is not directly tested in the cited literature — H053's mediation framing is therefore an extension, not a confirmation, of established findings.

**Gaps.** None of the cited papers test the 09:45–10:30 ET sub-window specifically, nor decompose its predictability into a part transmitted via the 09:30–09:45 ET bar versus a part flowing directly from longer-timeframe state. H053's mediation framing is therefore novel to that sub-window.

## B. Multi-timeframe technical features

- [Lo, Mamaysky & Wang 2000, *Journal of Finance* 55(4):1705–1765](https://doi.org/10.1111/0022-1082.00265) — non-parametric kernel-regression test of chart-pattern informativeness; methodologically validates pattern-based features rather than dismissing them as folklore.
- [Han, Yang & Zhou 2013, *Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis* 48(5):1433–1461](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0022109013000586) — moving-average timing as a cross-sectional anomaly; supports daily-timeframe distance-to-MA features.
- [Neely, Rapach, Tu & Zhou 2014, *Management Science* 60(7):1772–1791](https://doi.org/10.1287/mnsc.2013.1838) — technical indicators add to macro indicators in equity-premium forecasting; supports combining multi-timeframe technicals with regime/flow context.
- [Sezer, Gudelek & Ozbayoglu 2020, *Applied Soft Computing* 90:106181](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.asoc.2020.106181) — systematic review 2005–2019; baseline for ML/DL in financial time series.
- [Sezer & Ozbayoglu 2018, *Applied Soft Computing* 70:525–538](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.asoc.2018.04.024) — image-encoding of technical indicators for CNN trading; supports the LLM/multimodal arm's chart-snapshot encoding.

**Synthesis.** Multi-timeframe technicals carry incremental, non-overlapping signal; image encodings of indicator state are a published technique.

**Gaps.** No published implementation joins a daily-and-hourly-and-5/15-min snapshot with a fixed-clock-time intraday outcome window of the size H053 targets.

## C. Mediation analysis

- [Baron & Kenny 1986, *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 51(6):1173–1182](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.51.6.1173) — original mediation framework; cited for historical anchoring but not for inferential validity (its product-of-coefficients test is known to be underpowered, see MacKinnon et al. 2002).
- [MacKinnon, Lockwood, Hoffman, West & Sheets 2002, *Psychological Methods* 7(1):83–104](https://doi.org/10.1037/1082-989X.7.1.83) — Monte Carlo comparison of 14 mediation tests; product-of-coefficients with bootstrap CI is the recommended approach; Baron–Kenny three-step alone is dominated.
- [Imai, Keele & Tingley 2010, *Psychological Methods* 15(4):309–334](https://doi.org/10.1037/a0020761) — general framework spanning definition / identification / estimation / sensitivity for linear and nonlinear mediator-outcome pairs; companion to the `mediation` R package.
- [Imai, Keele & Yamamoto 2010, *Statistical Science* 25(1):51–71](https://doi.org/10.1214/10-STS321) — formal nonparametric identification under sequential ignorability + sensitivity analysis for unmeasured mediator-outcome confounding; the natural-effects sensitivity parameter ρ is the primary robustness check. (Round-1 lit-check correction: identification-under-sequential-ignorability is centrally developed here, not in IKT 2010.)
- VanderWeele 2015, *Explanation in Causal Inference* (OUP, ISBN 978-0199325870) — natural direct/indirect effects under exposure–mediator interaction; canonical reference for the counterfactual decomposition `TCE = NDE + NIE`.
- [VanderWeele & Ding 2017, *Annals of Internal Medicine* 167(4):268–274](https://doi.org/10.7326/M16-2607) — E-value sensitivity analysis for unmeasured confounding; carries directly to the financial-mediation context where unmeasured-confounding is the rule, not the exception.
- [Pearl 2001, *UAI*, arXiv:1301.2300](https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.2300) — graphical formalization of direct/indirect effects (do-operator + mediation). (Round-1 lit-check correction: the prior draft pointed at a UCLA tech-report URL that 404s; arXiv mirror is the canonical primary identifier.)

**Synthesis.** Modern mediation uses potential outcomes, not the original three-step procedure. Identification of natural direct and indirect effects requires sequential ignorability (no unmeasured exposure–mediator and mediator–outcome confounding) plus consistency and positivity. A sensitivity analysis (E-value or Imai–Keele–Yamamoto ρ) is the standard robustness check.

**Gaps and risks specific to financial time-series.** The published mediation literature is overwhelmingly applied to cross-sectional or panel data. In a 1-min-bar futures series with serial dependence, opening-window order flow, and unobserved overnight news, sequential ignorability is heroic; SUTVA fails when the same trader's positions span the mediator and outcome windows. The design.md must (a) frame the mediation block as **descriptive decomposition, not causal identification**, (b) report E-values, (c) acknowledge that a "significant NIE" is a statistical observation about predictability flow, not evidence that the opening bar *causes* the post-opening return.

## D. Chart-pattern archetype classification (for the table deliverable)

- [Lo, Mamaysky & Wang 2000](https://doi.org/10.1111/0022-1082.00265) — formal definition of head-and-shoulders, double top, etc., via kernel-smoothed local extrema; a defensible *pre-registered* categorization algorithm rather than a hand-picked taxonomy.
- [Bulkowski 2005, *Encyclopedia of Chart Patterns*, 2nd ed., Wiley, ISBN 978-0471668268](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Encyclopedia+of+Chart+Patterns%2C+2nd+Edition-p-9780471668268) — practitioner taxonomy; cited as descriptive prior, NOT as a primary inferential source. UNVERIFIED on rigorous backtest evidence — listed only as motivation for the archetype labels in the user-facing table.
- [Velay & Daniel 2018, arXiv:1808.00418](https://arxiv.org/abs/1808.00418) — CNN and LSTM classification of common chart patterns (head-and-shoulders, double-tops, etc.) on stock historical data; supports a learned-embedding approach to chart-pattern (Lo-Mamaysky-Wang taxonomy) classification, distinct from candlestick (Bulkowski-style) classification.
- For data-driven category construction without hand-picked bins, k-means / Gaussian-mixture clustering is the default: [Hartigan & Wong 1979, *Applied Statistics* 28(1):100–108](https://doi.org/10.2307/2346830). Number-of-clusters selection by BIC follows [Schwarz 1978, *Annals of Statistics* 6(2):461–464](https://doi.org/10.1214/aos/1176344136), already in use in the project's HMM toolkit per [ADR-0005](../../../docs/decisions/ADR-0005-hmm-regime-toolkit.md).

**Synthesis.** A pre-registered archetype set can be either (a) rule-based on quantiles of `(return, range, volume, OFI proxy)` of the opening 15-min bar — analogous to triple-barrier's PT/SL structure — or (b) learned by a clustering model on the training fold with BIC for k. H053 chooses (a) for interpretability *and* sets the rule thresholds at training-fold quantiles, not at the user's example numeric MNQ levels — the example levels (27,000 / 26,730 / etc.) are illustrative for the table format, not pre-registered constants.

**Gaps.** No primary-source backtest establishes that 5 archetypes is the right number for ES/NQ at the 15-min grid. The design.md treats k as a tunable selected by BIC + walk-forward CV; the labels (`bearish_engulf`, `indecision_doji`, etc.) are post-hoc display strings.

## E. Probability calibration for the lookup table

The headline deliverable is conditional-probability cells; calibration is required if any model arm produces continuous scores rather than direct empirical frequencies.

- [Platt 1999, "Probabilistic outputs for support vector machines," in Smola, Bartlett, Schölkopf & Schuurmans (eds.), *Advances in Large Margin Classifiers*, MIT Press, ISBN 978-0262194488](https://www.cs.colorado.edu/~mozer/Teaching/syllabi/6622/papers/Platt1999.pdf) — sigmoid post-hoc calibration. UNVERIFIED on a primary chapter-level DOI; URL is the canonical author copy.
- [Niculescu-Mizil & Caruana 2005, *ICML 2005*, ACM doi:10.1145/1102351.1102430](https://doi.org/10.1145/1102351.1102430) — empirical comparison of Platt vs isotonic regression vs raw scores; isotonic dominates for tree ensembles when training data is sufficient.
- [Zadrozny & Elkan 2002, *KDD 2002*, ACM doi:10.1145/775047.775151](https://doi.org/10.1145/775047.775151) — calibrating multiclass probability estimates.
- [Brier 1950, *Monthly Weather Review* 78(1):1–3](https://doi.org/10.1175/1520-0493(1950)078%3C0001:VOFEIT%3E2.0.CO;2) — Brier score; standard scalar evaluation of probability forecasts.
- [Murphy 1973, *Journal of Applied Meteorology* 12(4):595–600](https://doi.org/10.1175/1520-0450(1973)012%3C0595:ANVPOT%3E2.0.CO;2) — reliability + resolution decomposition of the Brier score; the design.md uses both as gating diagnostics for the lookup table.

**Synthesis.** Tree-based and linear models produce miscalibrated scores by default; isotonic regression on a held-out CV fold is the project's pre-registered calibration method. Calibration quality is reported via Brier score and reliability diagram. For the categorical-table arm, the cells are conditional empirical frequencies with bootstrap CIs — calibration applies to the model-derived probabilities, not the raw frequencies.

**Gaps.** Walk-forward calibration in financial time series is itself an open methodological topic; out-of-sample calibration drift is the rule.

## F. LLM-as-feature for intraday prediction

- [Lopez-Lira & Tang 2023, arXiv:2304.07619](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.07619) — ChatGPT for daily stock-price-direction forecasts from news headlines; baseline that LLMs carry signal at the daily horizon.

- [Wu et al. 2023, arXiv:2303.17564](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17564) — BloombergGPT; not open-weights; cited as the strong proprietary baseline.
- [Tetlock 2007, *Journal of Finance* 62(3):1139–1168](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2007.01232.x) — text-to-sentiment-to-returns; foundational that linguistic features carry pricing-relevant content.
- [Loughran & McDonald 2011, *Journal of Finance* 66(1):35–65](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2010.01625.x) — finance-specific lexicon; relevant if news-headline supplement is enabled.
- [Bommasani et al. 2021, arXiv:2108.07258](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.07258) — Stanford CRFM report on foundation-model reliability and brittleness; cited as the reproducibility-risk anchor.
- Prompt selection by data-driven optimization rather than hand-tuning, per the user-global rule for prompts reused >5 times: [Khattab et al. 2023 DSPy, arXiv:2310.03714](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.03714); [Agrawal et al. 2025 GEPA "Reflective Prompt Evolution Can Outperform Reinforcement Learning," arXiv:2507.19457](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.19457). (Round-1 lit-check correction: GEPA arXiv ID located; UNVERIFIED tag dropped.)

**Synthesis.** LLMs carry incremental signal on daily-and-longer horizons. The 45-min predictand horizon of H053 is shorter than anything published, so the LLM arm is a Tier-3 conditional in the design.md and does not gate the primary evidence-bar.

**Gaps.** No published work tests an LLM on a fixed 45-min equity-index futures prediction. Generalization, prompt brittleness, and reproducibility under deterministic replay (temperature 0, fixed weights, fixed tokenizer) are open.

## Lit-review-sourced risks for design.md

1. **Sequential-ignorability is not satisfied.** Time-series mediation under serial dependence and unobserved overnight news fails the Imai–Keele–Yamamoto identifying assumptions. The design must frame mediation as descriptive decomposition and must report an E-value robustness check.
2. **Heston-Korajczyk-Sadka periodicity is a confound, not a mechanism.** A positive predictand at 09:45–10:30 ET could be cross-sectional intraday seasonality rather than multi-timeframe-snapshot information. The design must include a benchmark arm that uses time-of-day fixed effects only; Sharpe-differential gate is *against this benchmark* in addition to the passive-long benchmark.
3. **Overnight reversal vs continuation.** [LPS 2019](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2019.03.011) finds same-period overnight continuation with cross-period reversal; [BKTZ 2012](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0022109012000270) finds overnight-to-day reversal driven by retail attention. The two are consistent (both involve overnight gain followed by intraday give-back) but produce different sign predictions for the post-09:30 ET intraday window depending on which mechanism dominates ex ante. The mediation decomposition must therefore not assume a sign on the opening-bar mediator's coefficient.
4. **LLM brittleness and non-determinism.** Bommasani et al. 2021 caution against treating LLM outputs as stable inputs; H053 must require temperature 0, pinned weights, fixed tokenizer, and a model-hash record in the ReproLog before the LLM arm enters the SPA family.
5. **Calibration drift in walk-forward.** Niculescu-Mizil & Caruana 2005 documents that isotonic dominates Platt for trees with sufficient calibration data; H053 must reserve a held-out calibration fold inside each walk-forward step and report Brier reliability before any calibrated probability enters the user-facing table.
6. **Multiple-testing in the table.** Five archetype × two outcome dimensions (direction × target zone) inflates the number of cells the user reads as evidence. The design must Bonferroni-control or report the joint Brier score, not per-cell p-values.
7. **SPA-family inflation across model arms.** Three model arms × paired-differential statistic each consume one slot in the project's universe-snapshot SPA. The design must register all three slots ex ante, not after observing per-arm Sharpe.

## Summary

H053's primary content is novel against published literature: the 09:45–10:30 ET sub-window predictand, the multi-timeframe snapshot at 09:45 ET, and the opening-15-min-bar mediation framing are not jointly tested anywhere I could verify. Each component has primary-source support — opening-window microstructure, multi-timeframe technicals, mediation methodology, calibration, walk-forward CV, SPA inference — but the combination is the contribution. The design.md is therefore framed as a pre-registered exploratory hypothesis whose evidence-bar passage would be the first published test, not a confirmation of an established result.
