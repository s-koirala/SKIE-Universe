## Domain 1 — Variance Risk Premium directionality

**Citation**: Bollerslev, Tim; Tauchen, George; Zhou, Hao (2009). "Expected Stock Returns and Variance Risk Premia." *Review of Financial Studies* 22(11):4463-4492. [doi:10.1093/rfs/hhp008](https://doi.org/10.1093/rfs/hhp008).

**Verification status**: VERIFIED. DOI resolves; SSRN preprint at [SSRN 948309](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=948309); Federal Reserve working paper at [federalreserve.gov/pubs/feds/2007/200711/200711pap.pdf](https://www.federalreserve.gov/pubs/feds/2007/200711/200711pap.pdf); abstract retrieved via Oxford Academic + RePEc.

**Verified abstract excerpt** (verbatim from Oxford Academic abstract retrieved 2026-05-05): "The difference between implied and realized variation, or the variance risk premium, is able to explain a nontrivial fraction of the time-series variation in post-1990 aggregate stock market returns, with high (low) premia predicting high (low) future returns. The empirical results depend crucially on the use of 'model-free,' as opposed to Black-Scholes, options implied volatilities, along with accurate realized variation measures constructed from high-frequency intraday as opposed to daily data. The magnitude of the predictability is particularly strong at the intermediate quarterly return horizon."

**Verdict on H054 framing**: **SILENT**. BTZ 2009 documents that the VRP predicts FUTURE quarterly aggregate equity returns (the predictand is the next-quarter S&P 500 return, predicted by today's VRP). The H054 framing is the converse statistical object: contemporaneous intraday session-level returns conditional on a session being classified into the HMM stress state. BTZ's predictand is multi-month-ahead; H054's is contemporaneous-intraday. While the directional sign of BTZ's prediction (high VRP → high future quarterly returns) is loosely consistent with the broad notion that "stress-period returns can be elevated," the paper does NOT support intraday session-level directional alpha conditional on HMM regime state.

## Domain 2 — Counter-cyclical risk premium

**Citation**: Cochrane, John H. (2011). "Presidential Address: Discount Rates." *Journal of Finance* 66(4):1047-1108. [doi:10.1111/j.1540-6261.2011.01671.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2011.01671.x).

**Verification status**: VERIFIED. DOI confirmed; SSRN preprint at [SSRN 1820084](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=1820084); NBER working paper [w16972](https://www.nber.org/papers/w16972); author's site at [johnhcochrane.com/news-op-eds-all/discount-rates](https://www.johnhcochrane.com/news-op-eds-all/discount-rates).

**Verified abstract excerpt** (retrieved 2026-05-05): "Discount-rate variation is the central organizing question of current asset-pricing research. I survey facts, theories, and applications. Previously, we thought that variation in price-dividend ratios was due to variation in expected cashflows. Now it seems that all price-dividend variation corresponds to discount-rate variation."

**Verdict on H054 framing**: **SILENT**. Cochrane 2011 is the canonical AFA Presidential Address surveying discount-rate variation in asset pricing. The paper documents that the bulk of price-dividend ratio variation corresponds to expected-return (discount-rate) variation rather than cashflow news, and that expected returns appear counter-cyclical at the BUSINESS-CYCLE / multi-quarter horizon. The paper is a SURVEY of asset-pricing horizons (typically monthly-to-multi-year); intraday HMM-stress-state directional alpha is NOT within the paper's scope. The broad notion of "counter-cyclical expected returns" Cochrane surveys is at the MACRO horizon, not the intraday HMM-classified-stress-session horizon. No support OR contradiction for the H054 framing at the intraday-session level.

**Caveat on lit-check completeness**: full-text retrieval of Cochrane 2011 was not completed in this Phase 0 session (PDF binary at [paulgp.com/speeches/cochrane_2011_afa.pdf](http://paulgp.com/speeches/cochrane_2011_afa.pdf) was retrieved but encoding was not text-extracted; full-text claim-by-claim verification deferred to a re-check pass under follow-up `P1-H054-COCHRANE-FULLTEXT-VERIFY`). The verdict above rests on the verified abstract + the project's prior knowledge of the paper as a quarterly-horizon survey.

## Domain 3 — Crisis alpha / CTA directionality

**Citation**: Hurst, Brian; Ooi, Yao Hua; Pedersen, Lasse Heje (2017). "A Century of Evidence on Trend-Following Investing." *Journal of Portfolio Management* 44(1):15-29. [SSRN 2993026](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2993026); [pm-research.com/content/iijpormgmt/44/1/15](https://www.pm-research.com/content/iijpormgmt/44/1/15).

**Verification status**: VERIFIED. SSRN preprint + JPM 44(1):15-29 confirmed; AQR-published version at [aqr.com/Insights/Research/Journal-Article/A-Century-of-Evidence-on-Trend-Following-Investing](https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/Journal-Article/A-Century-of-Evidence-on-Trend-Following-Investing).

**Verified abstract excerpt** (retrieved 2026-05-05): "We study the performance of trend-following investing across global markets since 1880, extending the existing evidence by more than 100 years using a novel data set. We find that in each decade since 1880, time-series momentum has delivered positive average returns with low correlations to traditional asset classes. Further, time-series momentum has performed well in 8 out of 10 of the largest crisis periods over the century, defined as the largest drawdowns for a 60/40 stock/bond portfolio." Studies 67 markets across four asset classes (29 commodities, 11 equity indices, 15 bond markets, 12 currency pairs) Jan 1880 → Dec 2016.

**Verdict on H054 framing**: **SILENT** on H054 specifically; **SUPPORTS** the broad notion of "stress-period directional alpha" for a DIFFERENT signal class (time-series momentum). HOP 2017 is the canonical crisis-alpha citation; it shows time-series momentum (TSMOM) earned positive returns in 8 of the 10 largest century-scale crisis periods (defined as 60/40 portfolio drawdowns — these are MULTI-MONTH events, not intraday HMM-classified stress sessions). The H054 first-hour ORB on intraday HMM-stress sessions is a fundamentally different signal class (open-range-breakout, not trend-following) at a fundamentally different horizon (intraday session, not multi-month crisis). The crisis-alpha tradition supports "stress periods can have directional alpha for the right strategy class"; H054 must establish empirically whether first-hour ORB qualifies, since HOP 2017 does not directly support it.

## Domain 4 — HMM intraday equity-futures

**Citations** (multiple checked):

1. **Hamilton, James D. (1989). "A New Approach to the Economic Analysis of Nonstationary Time Series and the Business Cycle." *Econometrica* 57(2):357-384.** [doi:10.2307/1912559](https://doi.org/10.2307/1912559). Verification: DOI confirmed (project's standing citation; not re-fetched in this Phase 0).
2. **Guidolin, Massimo; Timmermann, Allan (2007). "Asset allocation under multivariate regime switching." *Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control* 31(11):3503-3544.** [doi:10.1016/j.jedc.2006.12.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jedc.2006.12.004); [SSRN 940652](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=940652).

**Verification status**: VERIFIED for Guidolin-Timmermann 2007; full bibliographic detail confirmed via JEDC + SSRN + RePEc.

**Verified abstract excerpt** (Guidolin-Timmermann 2007, retrieved 2026-05-05): "We study asset allocation decisions in the presence of regime switching in asset returns. We find evidence that four separate regimes — characterized as crash, slow growth, bull and recovery states — are required to capture the joint distribution of stock and bond returns. Optimal asset allocations vary considerably across these states and change over time as investors revise their estimates of the state probabilities. In the crash state, buy-and-hold investors allocate more of their portfolio to stocks the longer their investment horizon, while the optimal allocation to stocks declines as a function of the investment horizon in bull markets."

**Verdict on H054 framing**: **SILENT** on intraday H054 specifically; **SUPPORTS** the broader notion that regime-conditional expected returns vary across HMM states. Guidolin-Timmermann 2007 fits a four-regime HMM on monthly stock+bond return data and finds optimal portfolio allocations vary by regime (counter-cyclical equity allocation in the crash state). The paper's regime-switching framework is at the MONTHLY horizon, not intraday; the regime states are macro-financial regimes (crash / slow growth / bull / recovery), not intraday HMM emission states from realized variance + first-hour sign + gap-size + DOW + ETH-pre-RTH-return + VIX. The H054 framing of "intraday HMM-classified stress sessions" is NOT directly supported, but the broader notion that "HMM regimes carry conditional expected-return information" is supported at coarser horizons.
