# Literature Review — H060 Phase 0

## Scope

Phase 0 lit-check for H060 — cross-futures time-series momentum (TSMOM) on a 4-asset CME-Globex retail-tier basket (ES, NQ, CL, GC). Per [user-CLAUDE.md `P1-CLAUDE-MD-LEDGER-AUDIT-DISCIPLINE`](../../../CLAUDE.md), every DOI is resolved to the claimed paper; author lists and journal-volume-issue-page details are verified against primary or quasi-primary sources (publisher landing pages where reachable; SSRN preprint metadata or public author-hosted PDFs where publisher pages are auth-walled). The project has a documented pattern of wrong-paper-DOI regressions (Harvey-Liu 2014, Hsu-Hsu-Kuan, Breiman-1996, Easley-Lopez de Prado-O'Hara-2012; per CLAUDE.md Phase J + Phase I audit-remediate trails) so every load-bearing citation in this memo is flagged with its verification source.

**Phase 0 verdict (binding before H060 `status: draft` → `status: designed`)**: **mixed — the canonical TSMOM anchor is verified but the empirical evidence base has degraded materially since Moskowitz-Ooi-Pedersen 2012 was published**. Two peer-reviewed primary papers (Huang-Li-Wang-Zhou 2020 *JFE*; Hutchinson-O'Brien 2020 *IRFA*) and a substantial practitioner record (the 2009-2019 "CTA winter" + the 2025 SG-Trend −18.7% one-year drawdown) materially weaken the "strong non-decayed edge" prior that a naive reading of MOP 2012 would support. H060's §1 hypothesis statement must reflect this honestly — the load-bearing claim cannot be "TSMOM is a strong well-established edge" but rather "TSMOM is a partially-decayed factor whose post-publication out-of-sample Sharpe is materially lower than MOP 2012's reported in-sample".

## §1 — Canonical TSMOM anchors

### 1.1 Moskowitz, Ooi, Pedersen 2012 *Journal of Financial Economics*

**Citation**: Moskowitz, Tobias J.; Ooi, Yao Hua; Pedersen, Lasse Heje (2012). "Time Series Momentum." *Journal of Financial Economics* 104(2):228-250. [DOI 10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003). **VERIFIED** via Elsevier ScienceDirect (DOI redirect to [linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0304405X11002613](https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0304405X11002613); title + journal + volume/issue confirmed). SSRN preprint at [SSRN 2089463](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2089463); public author copy at [docs.lhpedersen.com/TimeSeriesMomentum.pdf](http://docs.lhpedersen.com/TimeSeriesMomentum.pdf) (cert-expired session-of-record but URL load-bearing).

**Key findings (verified abstract excerpt + structural detail)**:

- Universe: <cite>"significant 'time series momentum' in equity index, currency, commodity, and bond futures for each of the 58 liquid instruments we consider"</cite> over 1985-2009. Per-asset Sharpe ratios fall in the 0.4-0.5 band; diversified-portfolio Sharpe ~1.0 — the √breadth multiplier from [Grinold-Kahn 1999 *Active Portfolio Management* 2nd ed. (McGraw-Hill, ISBN 978-0070248823, *practitioner*)](https://www.mhprofessional.com/) §"Fundamental Law of Active Management" attaches: `IR ≈ IC · √breadth`.
- Lookback grid tested: 1, 3, 6, 9, **12** (primary), 24, 36, 48 months. The 12-month signal is the canonical anchor; performance is reported across all values per Table 4 / Figure 1.
- Position-sizing rule: long if past-12-month excess return > 0, short if < 0. Position size scaled by `40% / σ_{t-1}` where 40% is the volatility target and σ_{t-1} is the ex-ante volatility estimate (cross-verified via search return citing the paper directly: <cite>"each position (long or short) is sized so that it has an ex ante annualized volatility of 40%"</cite>; <cite>"the choice of 40% is inconsequential"</cite>).
- Ex-ante volatility estimator: exponentially weighted moving average (EWMA) of squared daily returns with center-of-mass (COM) parameter <cite>"δ / (1 - δ) = 60 days"</cite>, i.e. δ ≈ 0.9836 (similar to a simple univariate GARCH-style estimator; the COM-60-day specification is what users implement, NOT a rolling 252-day standard-deviation).
- Rebalance cadence: monthly.
- Aggregate annualized excess return ~12%; aggregate Sharpe ~1.0 (1985-2009 in-sample).

The MOP 2012 §3 ex-ante-volatility specification is the canonical anchor for any H060 implementation; deviations from the COM-60-day EWMA must be justified empirically. The user's question prompt suggested "rolling 252-day exponentially-weighted" — this is **incorrect** as a verbatim MOP 2012 description; the paper uses COM-60-day not COM-252-day. Flagged for correction in the H060 design.md §1 mechanism description.

### 1.2 Hurst, Ooi, Pedersen 2017 *Journal of Portfolio Management*

**Citation**: Hurst, Brian; Ooi, Yao Hua; Pedersen, Lasse Heje (2017). "A Century of Evidence on Trend-Following Investing." *Journal of Portfolio Management* 44(1):15-29. [DOI 10.3905/jpm.2017.44.1.015](https://doi.org/10.3905/jpm.2017.44.1.015). **VERIFIED via SSRN preprint metadata** at [SSRN 2993026](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2993026) (title + author list confirmed; the *JPM* publisher landing at pm-research.com redirects to an OIDC authorization endpoint that blocks anonymous WebFetch — `P1-H060-CITE-DOI-VERIFY-JPM-PMRESEARCH` non-blocking re-fetch tracked).

**Key findings**: 137-year out-of-sample backtest (1880-2016) of a simple 1/3/12-month trend-following composite on 67 markets across equities, fixed income, commodities, currencies. The paper reports that trend-following <cite>"would have delivered strong positive returns and diversification benefits in each decade for more than a century"</cite> with positive returns in 9 of 10 stress events. Sharpe attributed in the practitioner literature at ~0.7-0.8 across the 137-year sample (lower than MOP 2012's in-sample Sharpe ~1.0 because the 1880-1984 leg is harder than the 1985-2009 leg). This is an AQR-authored paper; classified as *peer-reviewed but co-authored by the strategy provider* — supports the long-run-existence claim but does NOT independently verify post-2012 persistence.

### 1.3 Asness, Frazzini, Israel, Moskowitz 2014 *Journal of Portfolio Management*

**Citation**: Asness, Clifford S.; Frazzini, Andrea; Israel, Ronen; Moskowitz, Tobias J. (2014). "Fact, Fiction, and Momentum Investing." *Journal of Portfolio Management* 40(5):75-92 (40th Anniversary Issue). [DOI 10.3905/jpm.2014.40.5.075](https://doi.org/10.3905/jpm.2014.40.5.075). **VERIFIED** via SSRN preprint at [SSRN 2435323](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2435323) (title + author list + volume/issue/pages confirmed; semanticscholar [paper ca88d10966031d90ba807d3b448b2140c6e79844](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Fact,-Fiction,-and-Momentum-Investing-Asness-Frazzini/ca88d10966031d90ba807d3b448b2140c6e79844) corroborates). The paper enumerates and refutes ten momentum-investing myths; cross-asset momentum (including TSMOM) is treated as robust as of 2014.

Like Hurst-Ooi-Pedersen 2017, this is an AQR-authored paper at a *practitioner*-leaning peer-reviewed journal; treats supportive evidence but predates the major decay papers (§2 below).

## §2 — Decay, replication, and post-publication literature

This section is **load-bearing** for the H060 §1 hypothesis statement. The two papers below materially weaken the "strong non-decayed edge" prior.

### 2.1 Huang, Li, Wang, Zhou 2020 — TSMOM replication failure

**Citation**: Huang, Dashan; Li, Jiangyuan; Wang, Liyao; Zhou, Guofu (2020). "Time series momentum: Is it there?" *Journal of Financial Economics* 135(3):774-794. [DOI 10.1016/j.jfineco.2019.08.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2019.08.004) (via [ScienceDirect S0304405X19301953](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304405X19301953); SSRN preprint at [SSRN 3165284](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3165284)). **VERIFIED** via *JFE* RePEc entry [eee/jfinec/v135y2020i3p774-794](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinec/v135y2020i3p774-794.html).

**Key findings (verbatim from RePEc + ScienceDirect abstracts)**: <cite>"asset-by-asset time series regressions reveal little evidence of TSM, both in- and out-of-sample. While the t-statistic in a pooled regression appears large, it is not statistically reliable as it is less than the critical values of parametric and nonparametric bootstraps. ... the TSM strategy is profitable, but its performance is virtually the same as that of a similar strategy that is based on historical sample mean and does not require predictability."</cite>

**Interpretation**: Huang et al. 2020 do NOT refute the profitability of MOP 2012's diversified portfolio per se — they refute the **predictability mechanism**, showing that the cross-sectional t-statistic is inflated by pooling and does not survive a properly-designed parametric/nonparametric bootstrap. The economic profitability is preserved but attributed to a static long-vol-target with constant-sign positions, not to trailing-return-conditional sign decisions. This is the strongest peer-reviewed challenge to MOP 2012 to date.

### 2.2 Hutchinson, O'Brien 2020 — TSMOM and macroeconomic risk

**Citation**: Hutchinson, Mark C.; O'Brien, John (2020). "Time series momentum and macroeconomic risk." *International Review of Financial Analysis* 69:101469. [DOI 10.1016/j.irfa.2020.101469](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.irfa.2020.101469) (via [ScienceDirect S1057521920301137](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1057521920301137); WP at [UCC Centre for Investment Research](https://www.ucc.ie/en/media/research/centreforinvestmentresearch/wp/TSMEconomicRisk_Final.pdf)). **VERIFIED**. Note: published in *International Review of Financial Analysis*, NOT *Journal of Alternative Investments* (the user's prompt suggested JPM or JAI — both incorrect; flagged).

A companion earlier piece by the same authors — Hutchinson, M. C.; O'Brien, J. (2014). "Is This Time Different? Trend Following and Financial Crises." *Journal of Alternative Investments* 17(2):82-102. [SSRN 2375733](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2375733) — IS in JAI; the user's prompt may have conflated the two papers.

**Key findings**: data span 1921-2013 across 21 commodities, 13 government bonds, 21 equity indices, and currencies from 9 exchange rates. Returns to TSMOM are **state-dependent on the business cycle**: positive in both recessions and expansions but especially strong in expansions. Crucially, the 2014 JAI predecessor documents that <cite>"trend following average returns are less than half those earned in no-crisis periods"</cite> for ~4 years following the start of a financial crisis, which directly informs the 2009-2018 CTA-winter narrative documented in the practitioner record.

### 2.3 Baltas, Kosowski 2013 — volatility-estimation refinements + post-2008 correlation

**Citation**: Baltas, Nick; Kosowski, Robert (2013). "Demystifying Time-Series Momentum Strategies: Volatility Estimators, Trading Rules, and Pairwise Correlations." *Journal of Derivatives & Hedge Funds* 19(4):289-310. SSRN [SSRN 2140091](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2140091); chapter form at [Wiley *Market Momentum* DOI 10.1002/9781119599364.ch3](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/9781119599364.ch3). **VERIFIED**. Classification: *practitioner-leaning peer-reviewed* (JD&HF is an industry journal).

**Key findings**: (a) more efficient volatility estimators (Yang-Zhang, Garman-Klass) reduce turnover materially vs MOP 2012's COM-60-day EWMA; (b) introduces a correlation-adjusted variant that scales leverage by mean pairwise signed correlation — empirically, the <cite>"poor performance of time-series momentum strategies during the post-2008 period is explained by an increased level of pairwise correlations"</cite>. This is directly load-bearing for H060's 4-asset basket where ES-NQ correlation ≈ +0.85 reduces effective breadth below the naive √4 multiplier.

### 2.4 McLean, Pontiff 2016 — post-publication decay (factor-class baseline)

**Citation**: McLean, R. David; Pontiff, Jeffrey (2016). "Does Academic Research Destroy Stock Return Predictability?" *Journal of Finance* 71(1):5-32. [DOI 10.1111/jofi.12365](https://doi.org/10.1111/jofi.12365). **VERIFIED**.

97 cross-sectional anomalies; post-publication returns <cite>"58% lower"</cite> than in-sample; out-of-sample (pre-publication) returns 26% lower. NOT a TSMOM-specific paper but the canonical reference for the decay-magnitude prior. Applied to MOP 2012's reported Sharpe ~1.0, a literal 58% post-publication degradation would imply a post-2012 OOS Sharpe ~0.42; consistent with the practitioner record below.

### 2.5 Practitioner record (non-peer-reviewed, *practitioner*-tier)

- The **2009-2018 "CTA winter"**: SG Trend Index annualized return ~0.3%, Sharpe ~0, max-DD ~22% over the 10-year window. Multiple practitioner pieces document this; load-bearing as a strict empirical record even though the publication venue is industry, not peer-reviewed. [Man Group "Trend Following and Drawdowns"](https://www.man.com/insights/is-this-time-different); [AIMA "Dynamics of Dispersion"](https://www.aima.org/article/a-trend-following-deep-dive-the-dynamics-of-dispersion.html).
- The **2025 SG-Trend −18.7% one-year drawdown** through April 30, 2025 — the worst 12-month period in the index's 25-year history per the public press record.

These practitioner data points are NOT peer-reviewed inference but they are objective ex-post performance numbers that any honest H060 §1 must acknowledge.

## §3 — TSMOM construction details (MOP 2012-canonical)

For implementation in H060, the MOP 2012 §3 specification is the project-canonical baseline. Verified per §1.1 above:

1. **Ex-ante volatility estimator**: COM-60-day EWMA of squared daily excess returns. Formula: `σ²_t = (1 - δ) · Σ_{k≥0} δ^k · (r_{t-k} - r̄)²` with `δ = 60/61 ≈ 0.9836`. Annualized via `σ_t · √252`. The user's prompt suggested "rolling 252-day exponentially weighted" — this is **NOT** MOP 2012's specification and should be corrected in design.md §1.
2. **Lookback range tested**: 1, 3, 6, 9, **12** (primary), 24, 36, 48 months — NOT 1, 3, 6, 9, 12, 24, 48 as the user's prompt suggested (36 is included; 48 is the longest tested).
3. **Position-direction rule**: `sign(r_{t-1, t-12_excess})` — long if past-12-month excess return positive, short if negative.
4. **Position-size rule**: `position_size_t = (40% / σ_{ex-ante, t-1}) · sign(past_return)`. The 40% volatility-target is canonical-but-inconsequential (any constant rescales the overall portfolio leverage; not a substantive design choice).
5. **Rebalance cadence**: monthly. Daily rebalance is feasible but introduces material turnover costs; weekly rebalance is the Baltas-Kosowski 2013 sweet-spot recommendation.
6. **Per-asset vs portfolio Sharpe**: MOP 2012 reports per-asset Sharpe in the 0.4-0.5 band; 58-asset diversified-portfolio Sharpe ~1.0 in-sample (1985-2009). The √breadth Grinold-Kahn 1999 multiplier holds under low-correlation assumption; with correlated assets (e.g., the 9 equity-index futures in MOP's universe), the effective-breadth multiplier is materially less than √58.

## §4 — SKIE-Universe 4-asset basket (ES, NQ, CL, GC)

The H060 universe (ES, NQ, CL, GC) is a **deliberately narrow** 4-asset retail-tier basket. The MOP 2012 universe contains 58 instruments including ES + NQ analogues (S&P 500 + NASDAQ-100 futures), CL (NYMEX WTI crude), and GC (COMEX gold) — all four H060 instruments ARE in MOP 2012's universe. This is favorable for the prior but the SKIE basket is 7% of MOP's breadth.

**Cross-asset correlation behavior (informal practitioner record; load-bearing for §4 effective-breadth claim)**:

- ES-NQ (equity index pair): rolling 252-day correlation ≈ +0.85 to +0.95 over 1990-2024. Effectively a single risk factor with low effective breadth contribution.
- ES-GC: regime-dependent; rolling correlation ranges from -0.2 (risk-off equity-down / gold-up regime) to +0.3 (rate-cut regime). Average ≈ 0 over long samples.
- ES-CL: rolling correlation typically +0.2 to +0.4; commodity-beta to global growth. Episodically negative (e.g., 2014-2016 oil collapse) but most of the time positive.
- GC-CL: rolling correlation +0.1 to +0.3; both are dollar-denominated commodities so a USD-strength shock co-moves them.

A precise published study on cross-asset-futures correlations specific to this 4-asset set was NOT located in this Phase 0 sweep. The closest peer-reviewed anchor is [Brunnermeier, Markus K.; Pedersen, Lasse Heje (2009). "Market Liquidity and Funding Liquidity." *Review of Financial Studies* 22(6):2201-2238. DOI 10.1093/rfs/hhn098](https://academic.oup.com/rfs/article-abstract/22/6/2201/1592184). **VERIFIED** via Oxford Academic. The paper establishes that liquidity has commonality across securities and that funding-constraint shocks raise cross-asset correlations during stress — directly relevant to the Baltas-Kosowski-2013 finding that elevated post-2008 correlations degrade TSMOM efficacy in stress regimes.

**Effective-breadth correction for H060**: with ES-NQ ≈ +0.85, the equity pair contributes ~1.05 effective assets (not 2); CL and GC contribute approximately 1 each; total effective breadth ~3.1 not 4. Grinold-Kahn 1999 √breadth multiplier therefore ~1.76, not 2.0. Per-asset Sharpe of 0.4-0.5 (MOP 2012 in-sample, NOT post-publication) implies basket Sharpe ~0.70-0.88. After applying the McLean-Pontiff 2016 58%-post-publication-decay prior, expected post-2012 OOS basket Sharpe ~0.29-0.37 — i.e., consistent with the 2009-2018 SG-Trend Sharpe ~0 record within reasonable estimation error.

## §5 — Recommendation for H060 §1 hypothesis statement

Three load-bearing recommendations follow from the above:

- **Use canonical MOP 2012 (12-month signal, monthly rebalance, COM-60-day-EWMA vol scaling) as the primary specification, not a higher-frequency variant.** The Databento 1-min substrate enables higher-frequency exploration but the literature does not support a within-day TSMOM signal — every peer-reviewed result is at monthly grain or coarser. A higher-frequency variant (e.g., 60-day signal, weekly rebalance) is admissible as a §14 robustness exhibit, NOT as the primary specification. The 1-min substrate is useful for execution slippage modeling, not for the alpha-signal grain.
- **Separate the equity pair (ES, NQ) and commodity pair (CL, GC) as two sub-basket robustness exhibits** — consistent with the project's ADR-0019 barbell-positive-skew framing (if that ADR exists; assumed from user prompt — not separately verified in this lit-check). The high ES-NQ correlation collapses the equity-pair to ~1 effective asset; the GC-CL pair has more independent return content (~1.8 effective assets at +0.2 average correlation). Reporting the two sub-baskets separately makes the cross-asset-diversification claim auditable.
- **H_1 must be honestly framed as a partially-decayed-factor test, not a strong-edge test.** The §1 hypothesis statement should read approximately: "We hypothesize that the MOP-2012-canonical TSMOM signal retains a positive but materially-degraded predictive component on the 4-asset SKIE basket over 2015-2025 OOS, with expected basket Sharpe in the 0.2-0.4 range after accounting for McLean-Pontiff 2016 post-publication decay and Baltas-Kosowski 2013 post-2008 correlation-induced effective-breadth reduction." The contradicting papers (Huang et al. 2020 in particular) must be cited inline in §1; the survival-constrained primary metric vector per ADR-0017 (terminal-wealth-q05, Calmar-differential, profit-factor, R-multiple) is more appropriate than a Sharpe-primary criterion given how marginal the expected edge is.

**Contradicting literature flagged for inline §1 citation**: Huang-Li-Wang-Zhou 2020 *JFE* (predictability mechanism fails parametric/nonparametric bootstrap); Hutchinson-O'Brien 2020 *IRFA* (state-dependent on business cycle; underperforms ~4 years post-crisis); Baltas-Kosowski 2013 (post-2008 correlation increase explains poor performance); the 2009-2018 SG-Trend Sharpe ~0 practitioner record (10-year out-of-sample underperformance).
