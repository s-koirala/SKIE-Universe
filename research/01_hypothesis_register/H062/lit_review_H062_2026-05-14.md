The project's [`P1-CLAUDE-MD-LEDGER-AUDIT-DISCIPLINE`](../../../CLAUDE.md) discipline applies — known wrong-paper-DOI regressions in the project history include Harvey-Liu RFS 2014, Hsu-Hsu-Kuan, Breiman 1996, Easley-LdP-O'Hara 2012, Athey-Imbens 2019, Holmberg-Lönnbark, Auer EXP3.S, Browne 1999, TimeGAN, Assefa 2020, Besson-Kaufmann 2018, Dekel-STOC, and Stoikov-Saglam (every one inline-remediated under the relevant Phase trail).

**Phase 0 verdict (binding before H062 `status: draft` → `status: designed`)**: **mixed — the canonical channel-breakout anchor is *practitioner*-tier; the closest peer-reviewed validation literature for intraday Donchian / Turtle-style channel breakouts on retail-tier futures is sparse and the strongest decay evidence places these strategies in the "well-known and partially-decayed" regime**. The H062 §1 hypothesis statement must reflect this honestly — the load-bearing claim cannot be "Donchian breakout is a strong well-established edge" but rather "Donchian breakout retains a measurable but partially-decayed payoff with characteristic skew-positive structure on intraday futures; the load-bearing inferential test is whether MPPM(ρ=1) > 0 on the OOS test fold with stationary-bootstrap CI excluding zero". This is precisely the [ADR-0018](../../../docs/decisions/ADR-0018-regime-conditional-aggressive-growth-paradigm.md) §Context [Lo 2004 AMH](https://doi.org/10.3905/jpm.2004.442611) framing: strategy decay is the null, not the alternative.

## §1 — Channel-breakout / Turtle-system anchors

### 1.1 Donchian 1960 *Commodities Magazine* — original channel breakout

**Citation**: Donchian, Richard D. (1960). "Commodities: High Finance in Copper." *Financial Analysts Journal* 16(6):133-142. [DOI 10.2469/faj.v16.n6.133](https://doi.org/10.2469/faj.v16.n6.133). *practitioner-leaning peer-reviewed*; CFA-Institute-published. **R1 L1-006 fix**: title prefix "Commodities:" restored per the published FAJ archive; DOI back-assigned by CFA Institute now available.

**Note**: The popular attribution "Donchian invented the 20-day channel breakout in *Commodities Magazine* (1960)" is *practitioner folklore*; the verifiable Donchian publication record in the FAJ archive contains commodity-trading methodology pieces but no single founding paper specifying the modern N-bar high/low channel-breakout rule. The N-bar channel-breakout rule as canonical signal-construction was canonicalized in [Faith 2007 *Way of the Turtle*](https://www.amazon.com/Way-Turtle-Secret-Methods-Successful/dp/0071486646) (§1.2 below) and Donchian's contribution is best characterized as the moving-average + channel discipline lineage rather than a single founding equation. H062 cites Donchian via Faith 2007's canonical Turtle-system codification.

### 1.2 Faith 2007 *Way of the Turtle* — canonical Turtle-system codification

**Citation**: Faith, Curtis M. (2007). *Way of the Turtle: The Secret Methods that Turned Ordinary People into Legendary Traders*. McGraw-Hill Education. ISBN 978-0071486644. *practitioner*. Publisher page: [mhprofessional.com](https://www.mhprofessional.com/9780071486644-usa-way-of-the-turtle-the-secret-methods-that-turned-ordinary-people-into-legendary-traders-group). **VERIFIED** ISBN-13 via publisher catalog and WorldCat. (The user's prompt cited ISBN 0071486646; the verified ISBN-13 is 978-0071486644 — the 10-digit form 0071486644 is acceptable but the 8-digit truncation in the prompt is a typo.)

**Key construction (verified excerpts via published trader-community canonical references)**:
- **System 1**: 20-day breakout (`N=20`) with 10-day stop-out reversal. Long on close above 20-day high; short on close below 20-day low. Exit on 10-day reversal in opposite direction OR 2N ATR stop.
- **System 2**: 55-day breakout (`N=55`) with 20-day stop-out reversal. Long on close above 55-day high; short on close below 55-day low. Exit on 20-day reversal OR 2N ATR stop.
- **Stop convention (canonical "Turtle 2N")**: position stop at `entry ± 2 × ATR_20` where `ATR_20` is the 20-day Average True Range (Wilder smoothing per Wilder 1978; *practitioner*). The "2N" terminology is Curtis Faith's; N is the per-symbol ATR equivalent.
- **Unit-sizing**: 1 unit = `0.01 × account_size / (N × dollars_per_point)` (the original Turtle rule; equivalent to fixed-fractional 1% risk-per-trade per ATR-defined stop).
- **Pyramiding**: add up to 4 units at `0.5N` price increments above entry.

The Turtle system is the canonical *practitioner* N-bar channel-breakout codification; primary-source verification consists of (a) the published Faith 2007 book; (b) the Original Turtle Trading Rules document (a redistributed 1986 internal training document — *practitioner-historical*; not peer-reviewed). The Faith 2007 book and the Turtle Rules document agree on Systems 1 and 2 above.

### 1.3 Crabel 1990 *Day Trading with Short Term Price Patterns* — opening-range / short-window canonical

**Citation**: Crabel, Toby (1990). *Day Trading with Short Term Price Patterns and Opening Range Breakout*. Traders Press. ISBN-10 0934380171 / ISBN-13 978-0934380171. *practitioner*. **VERIFIED** ISBN via OpenLibrary [OL1611959M](https://openlibrary.org/books/OL1611959M/) + AbeBooks + Biblio cross-references (R1 L1-001 fix: prior draft had wrong ISBN 978-0934380102).

**Key construction**: opening-range breakout (ORB) — define a session's "opening range" as the first N minutes' high/low (most common N ∈ {5, 15, 30, 60} minutes; Crabel uses 5-min and 30-min); long on break of ORB-high, short on break of ORB-low. The Crabel ORB literature is the closest *practitioner* analogue to H062's intraday-channel-breakout family but is **time-anchored** (first N minutes of the session) rather than **rolling-bar-anchored** (rolling N bars over the whole session) — H062 uses the rolling-bar formulation. The H052a hypothesis in this project uses the time-anchored opening-range breakout per Crabel 1990 + Zarattini-Barbon-Aziz 2024 [SSRN 4729284](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4729284); H062 is the rolling-bar successor in the channel-breakout family.

### 1.4 Zarattini-Barbon-Aziz 2024 — ORB on QQQ (closest peer-reviewed validation)

**Citation**: Zarattini, Carlo; Barbon, Andrea; Aziz, Andrew (2024). "A Profitable Day Trading Strategy For The U.S. Equity Market." [SSRN 4729284](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4729284). *preprint-tier*; not yet peer-reviewed at time of this lit-check (status verified via SSRN abstract page). **VERIFIED** title + author list.

**Key findings**: 5-min opening-range breakout on QQQ from 2016-2023 produced **annualized return ~33%** with Sharpe ~1.7 in their reported simulation. Their universe is QQQ ETF; H062's universe is CME equity-index + metals futures, so transfer is partial. Cited as the closest peer-review-adjacent positive evidence for intraday channel breakouts in the equity-index space.

## §2 — Channel-breakout decay literature

### 2.1 Hsu-Kuan 2005 *Journal of Financial Econometrics* — trading-rule meta-evaluation

**Citation**: Hsu, Po-Hsuan; Kuan, Chung-Ming (2005). "Reexamining the Profitability of Technical Analysis with Data Snooping Checks." *Journal of Financial Econometrics* 3(4):606-628. [DOI 10.1093/jjfinec/nbi026](https://doi.org/10.1093/jjfinec/nbi026). **VERIFIED** via Oxford Academic.

**Key findings**: applies the [White 2000 *Econometrica* 68(5):1097-1126 DOI 10.1111/1468-0262.00152](https://doi.org/10.1111/1468-0262.00152) Reality Check and Hansen 2005 SPA to a universe of >7000 trading rules (including N-bar channel breakouts) on multiple US equity indices. **Verified abstract finding** (per academic.oup.com + IDEAS-RePEc + SSRN preprint metadata): "*significantly profitable simple rules and complex trading strategies do exist in the data from relatively young markets (NASDAQ Composite AND Russell 2000) but not in the data from relatively mature markets (DJIA and S&P 500).*" The distinction is **market maturity** (young vs mature), NOT market capitalization (large-cap vs small-cap). NASDAQ Composite (the closest published-paper analog to SKIE-Universe's NQ = Nasdaq-100 futures) is in the **SURVIVES-SPA category**, alongside Russell 2000. S&P 500 (closest analog to ES) is in the FAILS-SPA category. **2026-05-18 erratum**: prior wording stated channel breakouts "FAIL on large-cap Nasdaq Composite" — empirically backwards. See canonical correction at [`research/01_hypothesis_register/_erratum_hsu_kuan_2005_2026-05-18.md`](../_erratum_hsu_kuan_2005_2026-05-18.md). Cited as the canonical multiple-testing-corrected evaluation of N-bar channel-breakout rules at index level; the load-bearing message is that ALL such trading rules must be evaluated under SPA-corrected inference per [Hansen 2005 *J Bus Econ Stat* 23(4):365-380 DOI 10.1198/073500105000000063](https://doi.org/10.1198/073500105000000063), not under raw Sharpe — directly load-bearing for H062's §8 multi-testing correction.

### 2.2 Park-Irwin 2007 — meta-analysis of technical-analysis profitability

**Citation**: Park, Cheol-Ho; Irwin, Scott H. (2007). "What Do We Know About the Profitability of Technical Analysis?" *Journal of Economic Surveys* 21(4):786-826. [DOI 10.1111/j.1467-6419.2007.00519.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-6419.2007.00519.x). **VERIFIED** via Wiley Online Library.

**Key findings**: meta-analysis of 95 modern (post-1988) studies on technical-analysis profitability across stocks, futures, and foreign-exchange. Of 95 studies, 56 report positive results, 20 negative, 19 mixed. Foreign-exchange shows the most consistent positive evidence; futures markets are mixed; equity markets show declining profitability post-1988. Channel-breakout rules (Donchian-family) are among the most-studied; positive results concentrated pre-1990s; weakening post-1990. Cited as the load-bearing meta-analytic anchor for the §1 partial-decay framing.

### 2.3 Marshall-Cahan-Cahan 2008 — futures technical analysis (decay update)

**R1 L1-005 fix**: prior heading mistakenly said "2017"; verified year is 2008 (already correctly cited in body + References).

**Citation**: Marshall, Ben R.; Cahan, Rochester H.; Cahan, Jared M. (2008). "Can Commodity Futures be Profitably Traded with Quantitative Market Timing Strategies?" *Journal of Banking & Finance* 32(9):1810-1819. [DOI 10.1016/j.jbankfin.2007.12.011](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jbankfin.2007.12.011). **VERIFIED** via ScienceDirect.

**Key findings**: tests 7846 trading rules (including channel breakouts) across 15 commodity futures from 1984-2005 using a stepwise SPA + Romano-Wolf multiple-testing-correction framework. **None of the 7846 rules generate statistically significant profits after multiple-testing correction.** This is the canonical commodity-futures-decay result; cited explicitly in §1 as the contradicting peer-reviewed evidence that H062's hypothesis statement must reflect honestly.

The user's prompt suggested Marshall-Cahan-Cahan 2017 — the verifiable paper is **2008**, NOT 2017; corrected here.

### 2.4 McLean-Pontiff 2016 — post-publication decay (factor-class baseline)

**Citation**: McLean, R. David; Pontiff, Jeffrey (2016). "Does Academic Research Destroy Stock Return Predictability?" *Journal of Finance* 71(1):5-32. [DOI 10.1111/jofi.12365](https://doi.org/10.1111/jofi.12365). **VERIFIED**. Same paper cited by H060 §2.4 lit-review.

97 cross-sectional anomalies; post-publication returns 58% lower than in-sample; out-of-sample (pre-publication) 26% lower. Applied to Donchian/Turtle's mid-1980s peak-popularity-then-popularization timeline (Turtle program 1983-1988; Faith 2007 published peak-publicity), the McLean-Pontiff prior would project a 58% post-publication decay floor — consistent with the Marshall-Cahan-Cahan 2008 SPA-null result and with the broader Park-Irwin 2007 meta-analytic finding of declining profitability post-1990s.

### 2.5 Hong-Stein 1999 — underreaction-mechanism causal anchor (§1.3)

**Citation**: Hong, Harrison; Stein, Jeremy C. (1999). "A Unified Theory of Underreaction, Momentum Trading, and Overreaction in Asset Markets." *Journal of Finance* 54(6):2143-2184. [DOI 10.1111/0022-1082.00184](https://doi.org/10.1111/0022-1082.00184). **VERIFIED** via Wiley Online Library + JSTOR.

**Key claim**: gradual diffusion of private information among "news watchers" produces *underreaction* over short horizons that "momentum traders" can profit from, until "overreaction" sets in at longer horizons. The model produces predictions consistent with both cross-sectional momentum ([Jegadeesh-Titman 1993 *J Finance* 48(1):65-91 DOI 10.1111/j.1540-6261.1993.tb04702.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1993.tb04702.x)) and time-series momentum ([Moskowitz-Ooi-Pedersen 2012 *JFE* 104(2):228-250 DOI 10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003)). H062's §1.3 causal-mechanism annotation cites Hong-Stein 1999 as the *upstream* causal mechanism — the underlying behavioral phenomenon that makes channel breakouts informative when they fire (a breakout reveals that information has diffused enough to overcome the prior support/resistance level).

## §6 — Recommendation for H062 §1 hypothesis statement

Five load-bearing recommendations follow from the above:

1. **Frame H062 honestly as a partially-decayed-factor test**, NOT a strong-edge test. The §1 hypothesis statement should read approximately: "We hypothesize that the N-bar Donchian-channel breakout retains a measurable but materially-degraded predictive component on the 4-asset SKIE basket {ES, NQ, MGC, SIL} over 2020-2025 OOS, with expected basket MPPM(ρ=1) > 0 on stationary-bootstrap CI excluding zero." The contradicting peer-reviewed papers (Marshall-Cahan-Cahan 2008 *JBF*; Hsu-Kuan 2005 *JFE*; Park-Irwin 2007 meta-analysis) must be cited inline in §1.
2. **Cite Donchian via Faith 2007** (canonical Turtle codification), NOT directly to Donchian 1960 *Commodities Magazine* (the founding equation cannot be primary-source-verified at the canonical N-bar-channel level).
3. **Anchor the channel-N grid to Turtle Systems 1 + 2** (`N ∈ {20, 55}` daily) **geometrically rescaled to 5-min intraday cadence**: H062's grid {20, 40, 60, 120, 240, 480} 5-min bars = {100min, 200min, 5hr, 10hr, 20hr, 40hr} brackets the Turtle daily-N range under a session-time-equivalent rescaling. Single-shot N choices outside this range require empirical justification.
4. **Use MPPM(ρ=1) as primary inferential metric** per ADR-0018 D-1; Sharpe-family CI demoted to secondary per ADR-0017 §1.2. Primary survival-constrained metric vector (terminal-wealth-q05, Calmar-differential, profit-factor, R-multiple-mean) per ADR-0017 §3.
5. **Causal-mechanism annotation §1.3 = `hybrid`** — Hong-Stein 1999 underreaction model as the upstream causal mechanism (a price break of a multi-bar high/low reveals that buying pressure has overcome the prior support/resistance — a real information-diffusion event under the underreaction model); the parameterizations of channel-N, k_atr, kelly_multiplier are correlation-only refinements selected by Brier-score competition on calibration holdout (no per-parameter causal claim).

**Contradicting literature flagged for inline §1 citation**: Marshall-Cahan-Cahan 2008 *JBF* (channel breakouts NOT statistically significant after SPA correction on commodity futures 1984-2005); Hsu-Kuan 2005 *JFE* (N-bar channel breakouts survive SPA correction on **young markets** = NASDAQ Composite + Russell 2000; **fail** on **mature markets** = DJIA + S&P 500; per [2026-05-18 erratum](../_erratum_hsu_kuan_2005_2026-05-18.md) the prior wording inverting this finding has been corrected); Park-Irwin 2007 *JES* (meta-analysis: declining channel-breakout profitability post-1990s); McLean-Pontiff 2016 *JF* (58% post-publication decay prior on factor strategies generally).
