# Synthetic / counterfactual substrate augmentation — scoping memo

This memo proposes — but does **not** decide — a new research thread extending the SKIE-Universe walk-forward out-of-sample (OOS) evaluation protocol from a single realized historical path to a distribution of plausible paths generated by regime-respecting deep generative models (GAN / VAE / diffusion families). The thread targets a fundamental, currently-unaddressed limitation in the program's inferential apparatus: every OOS verdict to date conditions on the unique historical realization that the world produced over the test window, and so collapses a distribution-of-outcomes question into a point estimate. A decision-to-adopt would land as a future ADR; this memo's role is scoping and primary-source survey.

---

## §1. Motivation

Every OOS evaluation in the SKIE program — H050, H052a, H053 v3/v4 — runs against the one realized 2024-2025 ES/NQ price path that history actually produced. When H050 KPI report card v1 ([research/01_hypothesis_register/H050/H050_kpi_report_v1.md](../../research/01_hypothesis_register/H050/H050_kpi_report_v1.md)) reports the gated arm at −81% (ES) and −84% (NQ) realized over the 2024-2025 OOS fold with LW2008 differential CI excluding zero on the negative side, the program currently cannot distinguish three mutually exclusive interpretations:

1. *The strategy is structurally bad.* The HMM regime-gating layer destroys edge regardless of which realized path it would have faced — the −81% is close to the median of a counterfactual distribution.
2. *The strategy is moderate and we got unlucky.* The 2024-2025 realization is in the 5th percentile of bad paths; under a typical counterfactual the gated arm would land near flat.
3. *The strategy is moderate and the OOS path was anomalously catastrophic.* The 2024-2025 fold included a regime that the training fold had not visited; a different counterfactual draw would have placed the gated arm near or above the unconditional benchmark.

The Bootstrap-forward-projection block mandated by [ADR-0013 §3.1](../decisions/ADR-0013-permanent-exploration-no-archive-ninjascript-terminus.md) partially addresses an adjacent question — the *forward* 252-session distribution of $10K-baseline ending equity — but it does *not* address OOS evaluation itself. The held-out test fold IS the one historical realization; the bootstrap operates over that fold's empirical per-session return distribution, not over plausible counterfactual paths that 2024-2025 could have produced under the same data-generating regime.

The same N=1 collapse silently underlies the H052a "non-significant null" verdict (T_H052a CI covers zero on both ES and NQ; the LW2008 CI is over bootstrap replicates of the realized OOS path, not over counterfactual paths) and the H053 v3 "marginal" Sharpe-vs-bench verdict on all four arm × symbol cells. In each case, the inferential machinery returns a CI whose probability statement is "if we resampled this realized OOS path's per-session returns under stationary-bootstrap assumptions". The substantively interesting probability statement — "if the 2024-2025 OOS window had been an independent draw from the data-generating process" — is not what the CI covers.

Synthetic substrate augmentation would close this gap by training a generative model on the empirical substrate ([data/processed/vendor_legacy_1min_roll_adjusted/](../../data/processed/vendor_legacy_1min_roll_adjusted/), dataset_checksum `b3ee230a...`) and producing N synthetic OOS substrates that share the empirical substrate's stylized facts but constitute independent counterfactual realizations. Each synthetic path is then evaluated under the same orchestrator that produces the canonical KPI report card; the verdict becomes a *distribution* of T_H values, realized OOS equity outcomes, and forward projection ending-equity quantiles rather than a single point.

This is a fundamental limitation, not a marginal improvement. Every catastrophic OOS finding in the program to date is N=1-conditional in a way that the post-mortem literature explicitly warns about ([Assefa et al. 2020 ICAIF §3.2](https://doi.org/10.1145/3383455.3422554) "the failure of the single realized path"; cf. [López de Prado 2018 *AFML*](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Advances+in+Financial+Machine+Learning-p-9781119482086) Ch. 11.1 "the historical sample is just one of many trajectories the data-generating process could have produced"). The decision to adopt — or not adopt — synthetic substrate augmentation has first-order implications for how confidently the program can read its own KPI report cards.

## §2. Literature

Primary-source survey organized by generator family, with the financial-time-series stylized-fact validation literature and the failure-mode literature treated as separate domains. All citations verified to peer-reviewed proceedings or refereed journals where possible; preprints flagged explicitly.

### §2.1 GAN-based generators

- **[Goodfellow et al. 2014 *NeurIPS* "Generative Adversarial Nets"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661).** The foundational GAN paper. Min-max two-player game between a generator G(z) and a discriminator D(x); under sufficient capacity and training, G converges to the data-generating distribution. Anchor for *what a GAN is*; not directly applicable to time series without architectural augmentation because vanilla GANs sample i.i.d. and have no notion of sequence coherence.

- **[Yoon, Jarrett & van der Schaar 2019 "Time-series Generative Adversarial Networks (TimeGAN)" *NeurIPS 2019*](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/8789-time-series-generative-adversarial-networks).** The canonical sequence-aware GAN with explicit temporal-coherence loss. (Note: no canonical arXiv preprint exists; the previously-cited arXiv:1907.05321 actually resolves to Time2Vec by Kazemi et al. 2019, an unrelated paper — cite NeurIPS 2019 proceedings only.) Architecturally novel in combining an autoregressive supervised loss (predict-next-step) with the unsupervised adversarial loss inside a shared embedding space, addressing the failure mode that vanilla GAN training does not enforce temporal dynamics. Directly relevant: SKIE's 1-min ES/NQ bars are exactly the kind of multivariate sequence TimeGAN was benchmarked on (the paper's stock-price benchmark uses daily Google bars but the architecture is bar-frequency-agnostic).

- **[Wiese, Knobloch, Korn & Kretschmer 2020 *Quantitative Finance* 20(9):1419-1440 "Quant GANs: deep generation of financial time series"](https://doi.org/10.1080/14697688.2020.1730426).** Load-bearing peer-reviewed anchor for financial-time-series GANs. Uses Temporal Convolutional Networks rather than RNN/transformer backbones (faster training, parallel-friendly receptive fields), trains on S&P 500 daily log-returns 1993-2018, and validates output against Cont 2001 stylized facts directly (their §4: heavy tails, no autocorrelation in raw returns, autocorrelation in squared returns, leverage effect). Reports that Quant GANs reproduce all six tested stylized facts to within the empirical confidence band — the strongest published validation of a financial-GAN to date. SKIE's setting is closer in spirit to this paper than to TimeGAN's because SKIE's empirical bar series exhibits all the same stylized facts the Quant GANs framework was specifically engineered around.

### §2.2 VAE-based generators

- **[Kingma & Welling 2014 *ICLR* "Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6114).** Foundational VAE paper. Variational lower-bound on the data log-likelihood enables tractable training of a latent-variable generative model via the reparameterization trick. Compared to GANs: VAEs are theoretically grounded in maximum-likelihood, train more stably, but tend to produce blurrier (lower-fidelity) samples on image domains. The empirical financial-time-series literature has explored VAE variants (e.g., variational-RNN combinations) but with less peer-reviewed traction than GANs or diffusion. Relevant to SKIE as a stability-favored alternative if GAN training proves unstable on 1-min bar data.

### §2.3 Diffusion-based generators

- **[Ho, Jain & Abbeel 2020 *NeurIPS* "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239).** Foundational diffusion paper for the modern reverse-process formulation. Trains a neural network to denoise samples corrupted by progressive Gaussian noise; at inference time, generates samples by iteratively denoising pure Gaussian noise back to the data manifold. Strongest theoretical guarantees among the three families (no mode-collapse failure mode), at the cost of slow inference (typically 100-1000 denoising steps per sample versus GAN's single forward pass).

- **[Tashiro, Song, Song & Ermon 2021 *NeurIPS* "CSDI: Conditional Score-based Diffusion Models for Probabilistic Time Series Imputation"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.03502).** Conditional diffusion for sequence data; benchmarked on multivariate time-series imputation tasks with strong results versus GAN and RNN baselines. Architecturally builds the diffusion process over the time axis with a transformer-style score network. Relevant to SKIE because the *conditional* generation pattern (condition on a regime label, or on the first H sessions of an OOS fold, and generate the remainder) maps onto the project's HMM regime taxonomy and the OOS-fold-extension setting cleanly.

- **[Sattarov, Schreyer & Borth 2023 *arXiv* "FinDiff: Diffusion Models for Financial Tabular Data Generation"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.01472).** *Preprint; not yet peer-reviewed at the date of this memo.* Note: the arXiv ID requires verification at Phase-0 lit-check time — the planning conversation surfaced both 2302.01754 and 2309.01472 as candidates; the primary-source check should resolve which is the actual FinDiff paper. Treated as suggestive prior art rather than load-bearing anchor pending verification.

### §2.4 Stylized-fact validation

- **[Cont 2001 *Quantitative Finance* 1(2):223-236 "Empirical properties of asset returns: stylized facts and statistical issues"](https://doi.org/10.1080/713665670).** Load-bearing anchor for evaluating synthetic-data quality. Enumerates eleven stylized facts that any plausible model of financial returns must reproduce: (1) absence of autocorrelation in raw returns at short lags, (2) heavy tails (kurtosis well above the normal value of 3), (3) gain/loss asymmetry, (4) aggregational gaussianity (heavy tails attenuate at longer aggregation), (5) intermittency, (6) volatility clustering (positive autocorrelation of squared returns), (7) conditional heavy tails (residual heavy tails after volatility filtering), (8) slow decay of squared-return autocorrelation (long memory in volatility), (9) leverage effect (asymmetric volatility response to negative versus positive returns), (10) volume/volatility correlation, (11) asymmetry in time scales (volatility at coarser scales predicts volatility at finer scales but not vice versa). The Quant GANs paper validates against six of these explicitly; the synthetic-substrate-validation gate proposed below would mechanize this check on SKIE's generator output.

### §2.5 Pitfalls and known failure modes

- **[Assefa, Dervovic, Mahfouz, Tillman, Reddy & Veloso 2020 *ICAIF '20* (ACM International Conference on AI in Finance) "Generating synthetic data in finance: opportunities, challenges and pitfalls"](https://doi.org/10.1145/3383455.3422554).** Load-bearing for the *pitfalls* discussion. (Note: prior cite of arXiv:2002.12326 + ICML workshop was wrong — that arXiv ID resolves to Bica/Jordon/van der Schaar's continuous-intervention GAN paper. The Assefa et al. paper was published at ICAIF 2020, not an ICML workshop, with the corrected author list above.) Enumerates the failure modes: (a) mode collapse (GAN-specific; the generator learns to produce one plausible path repeatedly), (b) memorization (generator overfits and replays training segments), (c) distribution drift (generator distribution shifts away from the empirical as training proceeds), (d) stylized-fact violation despite point-statistic match (Wasserstein distance can look small even when long-memory volatility behavior is destroyed), (e) the deep-failure-mode that this memo's §4 calls out: synthetic substrate augmentation can produce *biased* counterfactual distributions if the generator is itself fit on the empirical substrate that the OOS fold is part of, leaking OOS information through the generator's parameter estimate. The workshop paper does not solve (e); it flags it as an open problem.

## §3. Proposed primitives

Three candidate primitives organized by ambition level. The proposal is that the program ladder up — start with the cheap baseline, prove the validation gate, then commit infrastructure to the peer-reviewed-best-practice tier only after the cheap baseline either succeeds or fails in instructive ways.

### §3.1 Tier 1 — Stationary block bootstrap as zero-shot synthetic substrate

The project's inference layer already includes Politis-Romano 1994 stationary block bootstrap with Politis-White 2004 automatic block-length selection at [src/skie_ninja/inference/bootstrap.py](../../src/skie_ninja/inference/bootstrap.py). Applied at the bar level, this produces a zero-cost synthetic substrate by resampling overlapping blocks of consecutive bars with geometrically-distributed block lengths. Cheap to compute; no new infrastructure; serves as the sanity-check baseline that any more elaborate generator must beat.

Expected weakness: bar-level resampling **destroys regime persistence** because the geometric block length is tuned for the autocorrelation structure of the return series, not for the regime-state sequence. The H050 v1 KPI report card §"Bootstrap-destroys-regime-persistence" caveat documents this explicitly — the gated arm's behavior depends on long-run HMM regime transitions that the bootstrap's typical block length (tens of bars) cannot represent. Tier 1 is the right diagnostic baseline but is not expected to produce a regime-respecting counterfactual distribution.

### §3.2 Tier 2 — HMM-conditioned synthetic bars

Sample from the project's existing HMM regime-inference layer ([src/skie_ninja/models/regime/](../../src/skie_ninja/models/regime/), [ADR-0005](../decisions/ADR-0005-hmm-regime-toolkit.md)) by drawing latent state sequences from the transition matrix and emission samples from each state's emission distribution. Architecturally trivial — the existing HMM is fitted causally per-fold per ADR-0005 §3, and Rabiner 1989 §III Algorithm 5 ("Generation of an observation sequence") gives the canonical sampling procedure. Preserves regime persistence by construction (the geometric dwell-time-in-state distribution is exactly what the HMM models). Minimal new infrastructure: a `sample_observation_sequence` method on the existing `GaussianHMM` class, plus an orchestrator hook to dispatch the walk-forward OOS evaluation N times against N synthetic OOS substrates.

Expected weakness: the synthetic substrate is only as faithful to the empirical as the HMM's emission distribution. The current `GaussianHMM` assumes Gaussian emissions in 1-dim feature space; the empirical bar-return distribution is heavy-tailed (Cont 2001 stylized fact 2), so Gaussian-emission HMM samples will *under-represent* tail events. The Tier-2 primitive is honest about this limitation and reports it as a validation-gate flag.

**Recommend Tier 2 as the immediate next-action** if the operator authorizes the thread. The cost is one feature on an existing class plus an orchestrator integration; the value is a regime-respecting counterfactual distribution that is strictly more informative than the Tier-1 baseline.

### §3.3 Tier 3 — Full TimeGAN / Quant GAN substrate generator

Peer-reviewed best practice per Wiese et al. 2020 and Yoon et al. 2019. Requires substantial new infrastructure: a neural network training pipeline (PyTorch or JAX; the project has no existing deep-learning dependency), BLAS / GPU coordination on Windows ([ADR-0009 BLAS thread-pinning](../decisions/ADR-0009-blas-thread-pinning.md) compatibility check required), hyperparameter selection protocol (Wiese et al. 2020 §3 enumerates ~12 architectural hyperparameters and ~6 training-protocol hyperparameters; full grid is infeasible — Bayesian search or pre-registered defaults are the candidates), and a Cont 2001 stylized-facts validation gate as a hard *pre-emission* check that the generator output must pass before any KPI report card is allowed to consume its samples. Tier 3 is the longer-term thread and would itself require a dedicated audit-remediate-loop trail per the project's standard discipline.

## §4. Open questions

Four open questions that the Phase-0 lit-check and any subsequent pre-registration would need to settle.

**(a) How many synthetic paths is enough?** The natural answer is a power-calibration analog of the existing project's `n_required_for_power_80` framework (calibrated empirically per `P1-H050-EMPIRICAL-POWER-CALIBRATION`). For terminal-wealth-q05 and Calmar-differential per [ADR-0017](../decisions/ADR-0017-survival-constrained-optimization-paradigm.md), the bootstrap-CI-width-as-a-function-of-N curve is empirically estimable from a pilot of, say, 100 synthetic paths, after which the operator can decide whether 1,000 or 10,000 is needed to bring the q05 CI width below an actionable threshold. Pre-registration of the path count *and* the actionable-threshold rationale is mandatory.

**(b) How to validate synthetic-substrate quality without overfitting validation to the empirical substrate?** Cont 2001 stylized-facts gate is the canonical answer — check that the generator reproduces the eleven stylized facts within empirical CIs. The deep issue is that the eleven facts are themselves estimated from the empirical substrate, so "passes the gate" is a *necessary* condition for generator quality, not a *sufficient* one. The literature (Assefa et al. 2020 ICAIF §4) does not solve this; the operational compromise is to treat the gate as a fail-closed pre-emission check and to flag every passing generator with a `synthetic-substrate-validation-gate: cont-2001-pass` annotation that is *not* interpreted as evidence of unconditional generator quality.

**(c) Does the synthetic-substrate distribution have an i.i.d. assumption embedded that the empirical financial-time-series violates?** Almost certainly yes — and this is the deepest failure mode. The bootstrap-CI machinery assumes the N synthetic paths are independent draws from the data-generating process. If the generator is fit on the empirical substrate (call this $\mathcal{D}_{\rm emp}$), then the conditional-on-$\mathcal{D}_{\rm emp}$ distribution of synthetic paths is what the CI machinery actually integrates over, NOT the marginal distribution of the data-generating process. The bias from this conditioning is unbounded in principle. Operational mitigations: (i) split $\mathcal{D}_{\rm emp}$ into a generator-fit fold and a generator-validation fold and gate generator quality on the validation fold; (ii) report the synthetic-CI alongside the empirical-CI explicitly and treat the difference as a diagnostic.

**(d) NinjaScript implementation implications per [ADR-0013 §5](../decisions/ADR-0013-permanent-exploration-no-archive-ninjascript-terminus.md).** Synthetic-substrate-OOS-only strategies have no clean NinjaScript terminus because they were never tested against real bars — the C# implementation in [ninjascript/strategies/](../../ninjascript/strategies/) is keyed to the realized historical substrate, and the §5 mandatory-implementation rule presupposes the realized substrate as the OOS fold. ADR-0013 §5.1 compliance for synthetic-augmented hypotheses is non-trivial: either (i) the synthetic substrate is treated as *supplementary* evidence and the realized-substrate OOS verdict remains the canonical artifact (so §5 fires as usual), or (ii) the synthetic verdict is treated as primary and §5 needs a §5.x amendment for synthetic-augmented hypotheses. This is itself a decision-deferred-to-future-ADR question.

## §5. Decision deferred

This memo *proposes* the synthetic substrate augmentation thread; the decision-to-adopt is deferred to a future operator-authored ADR (proposed integer block: ADR-0023) and an audit-remediate-loop trail at the time of that decision. The memo's purpose is scoping and primary-source survey, not commitment of program resources.

Concrete next steps if the operator authorizes the thread:

1. **Phase-0 lit-check** on the eight primary sources in §2, with particular attention to verifying the FinDiff arXiv ID and to the Quant GANs §4 stylized-fact validation procedure (Wiese et al. 2020's validation protocol is the closest published analog of what SKIE's Cont-2001 gate would mechanize).
2. **Tier-2c hypothesis pre-registration** at proposed entry H200 — a reserved integer block for the synthetic-substrate-augmentation research domain. The H200 entry would be a pilot pre-registration for a "TimeGAN-augmented H052a NQ unconditional ORB OOS evaluation": take the strongest empirically-realized cell from any existing hypothesis (the H052a NQ unconditional ORB cell at realized +10.61% / P(loss)=18.56% per the Phase-H KPI report card v1 §End-of-simulation table) and re-evaluate it across N=1,000 synthetic NQ paths. The Phase-0 milestone is the Cont 2001 stylized-facts validation gate on the generator output — fail-closed before any KPI emission is allowed.

Four follow-ups to register if and only if the operator authorizes the thread:

- `P1-SYNTHETIC-SUBSTRATE-PHASE-0-LITCHECK` — primary-source verification of the eight anchors in §2, with explicit attention to the FinDiff arXiv ID resolution.
- `P1-HMM-CONDITIONED-SYNTHETIC-BAR-PRIMITIVE` — the Tier-2 cheap baseline; `sample_observation_sequence` on the existing `GaussianHMM` class plus orchestrator integration.
- `P1-CONT-2001-STYLIZED-FACTS-VALIDATION-PRIMITIVE` — the validation gate; consolidated under [src/skie_ninja/inference/](../../src/skie_ninja/inference/) per the project's primitive-organization convention.
- `P1-H200-RESERVED-INTEGER-BLOCK-ADR` — the reserved-integer-block ADR to claim the H200-H299 range for synthetic-substrate research, mirroring the existing H050-H059 Tier-2b block convention.

The thread is *not* commitment-to-launch; the decision-to-adopt is deferred and the four follow-ups above are conditional on operator authorization.
